# Decentralised Disaster Relief Funding Platform

## Research Gap Analysis

Notable issues identified through our gap analysis on proposed solutions.

| Aspect | Existing Research Papers | Proposed System |
|---|---|---|
| **Identity Verification** | Victims are verified using just Geo Location. Scammers can easily bypass this authentication and just qualify for aid | Uses privacy-preserving identity and location verification through Zero-Knowledge Proofs without revealing personal information. |
| **Governance Model** | Relief operations are controlled by centralized or semi-centralized authorities such as NGOs or governments. | Implements DAO-based decentralized governance where the community proposes and votes on relief campaigns. DAO's ensure democratic fund management |
| **Fund Disbursement** | Blockchain is mainly used for tracking donations; fund distribution is manual or delayed. | Automated smart contracts instantly release funds to verified victims without intermediaries. |
| **Accessibility** | Assumes English literacy, technical knowledge, and access to formal identification. | Provides a multilingual AI assistance support enabling easy interaction for disaster victims in local languages. |
| **Decision Support** | Relies on static data and historical transaction records. | Uses AI-generated real-time disaster summaries to support informed DAO decision-making. |
| **User Experience** | Systems are backend-focused with poor onchain user experience. | Offers a consumer-friendly onchain experience with smooth onboarding and low transaction costs. |
| **Donor Engagement** | Donors can only track transactions with no incentive mechanism. | Introduces NFT-based donor rewards to encourage long-term participation. |
