## 2. Blockchain Technology Model to Increase Transparency in the Distribution of Aid to Disaster Victims

**Authors:** Inayatulloh, Betley Heru Susanto, Nadila Setiabudiarto, Wiza Teguh, Erin, Ainun Jariyah
**Published:** ICEECIT 2024, IEEE — Bina Nusantara University, Jakarta, Indonesia

### Summary

This paper addresses a critical problem in Indonesia's disaster relief system: the lack of transparency and accountability in distributing aid to natural disaster victims using conventional, centralized approaches. Donors have no real-time visibility into how funds are allocated, and misuse of relief funds is a recurring concern.

The authors propose a blockchain-based model using the **Hyperledger Fabric** framework. The model incorporates five core components: a registration process, smart contracts, transactions, peer-to-peer validation, and a blockchain network.

**Proposed Model Highlights:**

- All parties (donors, government agencies, village heads, district heads) must register as nodes on the blockchain network.
- Aid transactions (e.g., TrAid01, TrAid02, TrAid03) go through peer-to-peer validation before being stored immutably on the chain.
- Donor UI allows submission of cash and goods aid with supporting documents; Village UI enables recipients to validate received aid.
- Each block stores a transaction hash, nonce, previous block hash, and block hash — ensuring a tamper-proof audit trail.

**Conclusion:** The peer-to-peer validation model ensures that all actors in the aid distribution chain have transparent, valid, and real-time access to transaction data, significantly reducing the risk of fund misuse and improving accountability.
