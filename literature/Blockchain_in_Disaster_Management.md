## 1. Blockchain in Disaster Management: A Systematic Review of Emerging Frameworks, Applications, and Future Directions

**Authors:** Pradeep Nazerath, Pramod Uppoor, Bhandary Pranith Laxman, Shivamani M Nayak, Shetty Gaurav Jagadeesha
**Published:** ICIMIA-2025, IEEE
<https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/11200906>

### Summary

This paper presents a systematic review of ten peer-reviewed studies examining how blockchain technology can improve disaster management across various stages — preparedness, response, recovery, and mitigation.

The review covers a wide range of blockchain applications including UAV swarm coordination, vehicular forensics, emergency service dispatch, public safety microservices, and inter-agency knowledge sharing. Key frameworks analyzed include the BEST (Blockchain-Enabled Secure and Trusted) architecture built on Hyperledger Fabric, and BlendMAS, a decentralized microservices architecture for public safety.

**Key Findings:**

- **Technical Capabilities:** Blockchain's core strengths — immutability, decentralization, and smart contract automation — make it well-suited for high-stakes disaster scenarios where data integrity and rapid decision-making are critical.
- **Functional Benefits:** Systems demonstrated measurable improvements in emergency response time, UAV swarm autonomy, forensic data authenticity, and stakeholder coordination.
- **Challenges:** Scalability bottlenecks with PoW and PBFT consensus algorithms, high energy consumption, interoperability with legacy systems, and the absence of regulatory frameworks remain significant barriers.
- **Emerging Trends:** Growing interest in DAOs for democratic fund management, NFTs for event authentication, and hybrid consensus protocols (DPoS + PBFT) to balance throughput and security.

The paper concludes that blockchain is evolving from an experimental technology into foundational infrastructure for transparent, resilient, and trustworthy emergency ecosystems — but achieving this requires coordinated action from technologists, policymakers, and humanitarian agencies.
